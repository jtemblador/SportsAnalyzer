Introduction: Background and Significance

Over time, simple macines have become better and better at solve complex problems. It has now
become possible to develop and model a simple statistical tracking tool to gauge player
performance by predictive modeling systems. However, most current platforms provide limited
transparency in their prediction methods, and offer subjective (and often unfair) letter grades
rather than probabilistic assessments. While also relying on cloud-based systems that lack
reproducibility and speed for intensive analysis.

This project addresses a sports analytics gap by developing a local-first NFL player
performance prediction system that combines machine learning algorithms with probabilistic
forecasting to predict individual player statistics and game performance. The significance of this
work extends beyond entertainment value.

Prior Work by Others

The field of sports analytics has seen significant development over the past two decades, with
several notable approaches to player performance prediction that this project builds upon while
introducing key innovations.

Existing Methodologies:

Team-Level Rating Systems: Multiple established systems focus primarily on team-level
predictions. The Elo rating system, originally developed for chess, has been adapted for NFL
team ratings by organizations like FiveThirtyEight. Similarly, Massey-Colley ranking systems
provide team strength assessments. However, these approaches are limited focusing on game
outcomes rather than individual player performance metrics.

Fantasy Sports Platforms: Commercial platforms like ESPN, Yahoo Fantasy, and DraftKings
provide player projections, but these systems suffer from several limitations: they typically
output subjective letter grades (A-F) rather than probabilistic assessments, and offer no
transparency into their prediction methodologies, and provide no uncertainty quantification or
confidence intervals.

Academic Research: The NFL Big Data Bowl competitions have produced various approaches
to player tracking and performance prediction, utilizing advanced metrics like Expected Points
Added (EPA) and success rate. Research by the nflverse community has established
standardized datasets and statistical frameworks, particularly through the nflfastR package in
R.

Probabilistic Player-Level Forecasting: Unlike existing systems that focus on team
outcomes or provide subjective grades, this project implements probabilistic forecasting
at the individual player level, outputting P-Over-Baseline (POB) probabilities and
Expected Value Over Baseline (EVOB) metrics with proper uncertainty quantification.
Local-First Architecture: Most existing systems rely on cloud-based APIs with rate
limits and connectivity dependencies. This project implements a local-first approach
using columnar storage (DuckDB + Parquet) for maximum performance and
reproducibility.
Explainable AI Integration: Current platforms provide predictions without
explanations. This project combines SHAP-based feature importance analysis with GPT-
powered natural language explanations, making complex ML models interpretable to end
users.

WHAT IS PPR (POINTS PER RECEPTION)?
================================================================================

PPR = "Points Per Reception" scoring format
- Players get 1 bonus point for EACH reception/catch
- Rewards pass-catching backs, slot receivers, and possession receivers
- Makes players with high reception counts valuable even without big yardage
- Most popular fantasy scoring format (vs Standard or Half-PPR)

EXAMPLE:
Player A: 5 catches for 50 yards = 5 (receptions) + 5 (yards) = 10 PPR points
Player B: 2 catches for 50 yards = 2 (receptions) + 5 (yards) = 7 PPR points
→ Player A scores higher despite same yardage (rewarded for volume)

QUARTERBACK (QB)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PASSING:
• Passing Yards: 0.04 points per yard (1 point per 25 yards)
• Passing Touchdowns: 4 points per TD
• Interceptions: -2 points per INT

RUSHING:
• Rushing Yards: 0.1 points per yard (1 point per 10 yards)
• Rushing Touchdowns: 6 points per TD

PENALTIES:
• Fumbles Lost: -2 points per fumble
  (includes sack fumbles lost, rushing fumbles lost)

FORMULA:
fantasy_points_ppr =
    (passing_yards × 0.04) +
    (passing_tds × 4) +
    (passing_interceptions × -2) +
    (rushing_yards × 0.1) +
    (rushing_tds × 6) +
    (fumbles_lost × -2)

EXAMPLE - Matthew Stafford (Week 13, 2025):
  243 passing yards × 0.04 = 9.72
  2 passing TDs × 4 = 8.00
  2 interceptions × -2 = -4.00
  -1 rushing yards × 0.1 = -0.10
  1 fumble lost × -2 = -2.00
  ─────────────────────────
  TOTAL = 11.62 points ✓

WHY THIS SCORING:
- Passing is worth less than rushing (0.04 vs 0.1) because it's easier
- Passing TDs worth 4 (vs 6 for rushing/receiving) for same reason
- Interceptions and fumbles heavily penalized (-2 each)
- Mobile QBs (Lamar Jackson, Josh Allen) benefit from rushing bonuses

RUNNING BACK (RB)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RUSHING:
• Rushing Yards: 0.1 points per yard (1 point per 10 yards)
• Rushing Touchdowns: 6 points per TD

RECEIVING (PPR):
• Receptions: 1 point per catch ← PPR BONUS!
• Receiving Yards: 0.1 points per yard (1 point per 10 yards)
• Receiving Touchdowns: 6 points per TD

PENALTIES:
• Fumbles Lost: -2 points per fumble

FORMULA:
fantasy_points_ppr =
    (rushing_yards × 0.1) +
    (rushing_tds × 6) +
    (receptions × 1.0) +        ← PPR BONUS
    (receiving_yards × 0.1) +
    (receiving_tds × 6) +
    (fumbles_lost × -2)

EXAMPLE - Derrick Henry (Week 13, 2025):
  60 rushing yards × 0.1 = 6.00
  1 rushing TD × 6 = 6.00
  1 reception × 1.0 = 1.00 ← PPR BONUS
  44 receiving yards × 0.1 = 4.40
  0 receiving TDs × 6 = 0.00
  ─────────────────────────
  TOTAL = 17.40 points ✓

WHY THIS SCORING:
- RBs score from BOTH rushing and receiving
- PPR format makes pass-catching backs elite (Christian McCaffrey, Alvin Kamara)
- Pure rushers (Derrick Henry) still valuable due to volume
- 1 reception = 10 yards (major value boost for targets)

WIDE RECEIVER (WR)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RECEIVING (PPR):
• Receptions: 1 point per catch ← PPR BONUS!
• Receiving Yards: 0.1 points per yard (1 point per 10 yards)
• Receiving Touchdowns: 6 points per TD

RUSHING (rare but happens):
• Rushing Yards: 0.1 points per yard
• Rushing Touchdowns: 6 points per TD

PENALTIES:
• Fumbles Lost: -2 points per fumble

FORMULA:
fantasy_points_ppr =
    (receptions × 1.0) +        ← PPR BONUS
    (receiving_yards × 0.1) +
    (receiving_tds × 6) +
    (rushing_yards × 0.1) +     ← Trick plays
    (rushing_tds × 6) +
    (fumbles_lost × -2)

EXAMPLE - Davante Adams (Week 13, 2025):
  4 receptions × 1.0 = 4.00 ← PPR BONUS
  58 receiving yards × 0.1 = 5.80
  2 receiving TDs × 6 = 12.00
  ─────────────────────────
  TOTAL = 21.80 points ✓

WHY THIS SCORING:
- PPR massively benefits slot receivers and possession guys (10+ targets/game)
- Volume is king: 8 catches for 60 yards = 14 pts (better than 3 for 90 = 12 pts)
- TDs are huge but unpredictable (6 points = 60 yards!)
- Some WRs get rushing attempts (Deebo Samuel, Tyreek Hill) = bonus points

KICKER (K)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FIELD GOALS:
• Field Goal Made: 3 points per FG
  (Some leagues have distance bonuses: 50+ yards = 5 pts, but standard is 3)

EXTRA POINTS:
• PAT (Point After Touchdown) Made: 1 point per PAT

PENALTIES:
• Missed FG/PAT: Typically 0 points (some leagues give -1 or -2)

FORMULA (Standard):
fantasy_points =
    (fg_made × 3) +
    (pat_made × 1)

EXAMPLE - Justin Tucker (hypothetical):
  3 field goals made × 3 = 9.00
  4 PATs made × 1 = 4.00
  ─────────────────────────
  TOTAL = 13.00 points

NOTE: Kicker scoring in our dataset
- The nflverse raw data has fantasy_points_ppr = 0.0 for all kickers
- This is a known data limitation in the nflverse API
- Kicker predictions likely use calculated fantasy points based on FG/PAT
- Kickers are the simplest position to predict (consistent across all versions)

WHY THIS SCORING:
- Kickers depend entirely on team offense reaching the red zone
- High-scoring teams = more PATs and FG attempts
- Weather affects kickers (wind, rain reduce accuracy)
- Most predictable fantasy position (low variance)

KEY INSIGHTS FOR FANTASY PREDICTION
================================================================================

1. PPR BONUS IS MASSIVE
   • 1 reception = 10 yards of value
   • 10-catch game = 100 yards in points (before actual yardage!)
   • Makes possession receivers (slot guys) super valuable
   • Example: Zach Ertz's 10 receptions = 10 points before ANY yardage

2. VOLUME > EFFICIENCY
   • Player A: 8 catches, 60 yards, 0 TD = 14 points
   • Player B: 3 catches, 90 yards, 0 TD = 12 points
   • Player A wins despite lower yards-per-catch efficiency
   • This is why V3's efficiency metrics (RACR, PACR) FAILED
   • Prediction must focus on opportunity/volume, not talent/efficiency

3. TOUCHDOWNS ARE RANDOM BUT VALUABLE
   • 1 TD = 6 points = 60 yards!
   • TDs are the hardest stat to predict (goal-line variance)
   • Red zone touches = best TD predictor
   • This is why R² can't exceed ~0.50 (inherent TD randomness)

4. POSITION SCORING DIFFERENCES
   • QBs: Passing cheaper than rushing (0.04 vs 0.1 per yard)
   • RBs: Dual-threat (rush + receive) = highest floors
   • WRs: PPR dependent, target share critical
   • TEs: Elite TEs are WRs, blocking TEs are worthless
   • Ks: Team offense dependent, weather matters

5. VEGAS LINES PREDICT VOLUME
   • High team total = more offensive plays = more opportunity
   • Spread predicts game script:
     - Trailing teams PASS more (QB/WR boost)
     - Leading teams RUSH more (RB boost)
   • Over/under predicts pace (fast = more plays)
   • This is why V4's Vegas features crushed V3's EPA metrics

HOW OUR MODEL PREDICTS FANTASY SCORES
================================================================================

PREDICTION PROCESS:

1. FEATURE ENGINEERING (50 features):
   ✓ Rolling averages of all stats (decay=0.85, emphasizes recent 3 games)
   ✓ Variance metrics (identifies boom/bust players)
   ✓ Trend features (hot/cold streaks)
   ✓ Vegas betting lines (spreads, totals, implied scores)
   ✓ Opponent defense rankings
   ✓ Usage metrics (target share, snap counts)

2. MODEL TRAINING (3 models per stat):
   ✓ POB: Probability of beating baseline (binary classification)
   ✓ EVOB: Expected deviation from baseline (regression)
   ✓ STAT: Direct stat prediction (regression)
   ✓ Ensemble of 4 algorithms (CatBoost, XGBoost, LightGBM, RF)

3. FANTASY SCORE CALCULATION:
   Option A: Predict fantasy_points_ppr directly
   - Model learns from historical fantasy scores
   - Automatically weighs important stats (TDs, receptions)

   Option B: Predict individual stats, then calculate
   - Predict: passing_yards, passing_tds, interceptions, rushing_yards, etc.
   - Apply formula: (yards × 0.04) + (tds × 4) + (int × -2) + ...
   - More interpretable but more complex

   OUR APPROACH: Option A (direct fantasy_points_ppr prediction)
   - Simpler, lets model learn stat importance
   - Fantasy score already calculated in raw data (nflverse API)
   - Model predicts deviation from rolling average baseline

4. CONFIDENCE INTERVALS:
   ✓ Ensemble variance captures uncertainty
   ✓ Lower bound = mean - 1.645 × std (10th percentile)
   ✓ Upper bound = mean + 1.645 × std (90th percentile)
   ✓ Wider interval = boom/bust player (high variance)

WHY FANTASY SCORING MATTERS FOR MODEL DESIGN
================================================================================

1. PPR REWARDS VOLUME → MODEL MUST PREDICT OPPORTUNITY
   • Target share > route efficiency
   • Snap count > yards per route run
   • Red zone touches > red zone efficiency
   • This is why V4's Vegas features (volume predictors) > V3's EPA (efficiency)

2. TOUCHDOWNS ARE RANDOM → CONFIDENCE INTERVALS CRITICAL
   • TD variance is the #1 source of prediction error
   • Wide receivers: 3 for 90 yards = 12 pts, but add 1 TD = 18 pts
   • Confidence intervals account for TD variance
   • Example: [15.7 - 20.7] bracket assumes 0-1 TD range

3. POSITION DIFFERENCES → POSITION-SPECIFIC HYPERPARAMETERS
   • QBs: Complex (game script, trailing/leading behavior) → depth=9
   • RBs: Predictable (volume-based) → standard depth=7
   • TEs: Mixed role (blocker vs receiver) → simpler depth=6
   • Ks: Simple (team offense) → depth=3
   • This is a KEY V4 innovation

4. RECEPTIONS = FLOOR → VARIANCE FEATURES DISTINGUISH PLAYERS
   • Possession receiver: 8 catches, 60 yards = 14 pt floor
   • Deep threat: 3 catches, 90 yards = 12 pts, but high variance
   • V2 variance features identify boom/bust players
   • Critical for DFS strategy (cash games vs tournaments)

next steps:
	1. Rolling Averages with Exponential Decay 
		- EX: weighted_avg = (24.5*1.0 + 18.2*0.9 + 32.1*0.81 + 15.7*0.729) / (1.0 + 0.9 + 0.81 + 0.729)
	2. Opponent Strength Adjustments (100 rush yards vs. the #1 run defense >> 100 yards vs. the #32 defense.)
		1. Calculate league average rushing yards allowed per game (e.g., 120 yards)
		2. Find opponent's average (e.g., KC allows 95 yards = strong defense)
		3. Adjustment factor = opponent_avg / league_avg = 95/120 = 0.79
		4. Adjusted baseline = player_baseline * adjustment_factor
	3. Usage Metrics (Snap Share / Target Share) 
			Week 1: 8 targets out of 35 team passes = 22.9% target share
			Week 2: 10 targets out of 40 team passes = 25.0%
			Week 3: 12 targets out of 38 team passes = 31.6%
		- Trend: Target share is increasing → player becoming more involved in offense → predict higher future performance.
	4. POB (Probability Over Baseline) (binary classifier: Will this player beat their average)
		- Player's rolling avg: 15.2 fantasy points (IS IT BEST TO USE FANTASY POINTS FOR CALC ROLLING AVG?)
		- Model predicts: 72% chance they score MORE than 15.2 points
		- POB = 0.72 (72% probability)
	5. EVOB (Expected Value Over Baseline) (regression model: By HOW MUCH will they beat/miss their average)
	
	Training Data Questions
	- We will train separate models on 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8 years of data to see where the optimal training years set is. 
	
	ML Models to Train:
		For POB (Binary Classification):
			- Logistic Regression (simple baseline)
			- XGBoost Classifier (probably best)
			- LightGBM Classifier (alternative, faster than XGBoost)

		For EVOB (Regression):
			- Linear Regression (simple baseline)
			- XGBoost Regressor (probably best)

================================================================================
KEY INSIGHTS DISCOVERED
================================================================================

1. PPR SCORING IS FUNDAMENTAL
   - 1 reception = 1 point = 10 yards of value
   - Volume > efficiency for fantasy success
   - Example: 8 catches, 60 yards = 14 pts
               3 catches, 90 yards = 12 pts
   - First player wins despite lower efficiency

2. WHY V3 FAILED EXPLAINED
   - V3 used efficiency metrics (EPA, RACR, PACR, CPOE, WOPR)
   - These measure HOW WELL players perform
   - Fantasy rewards HOW MUCH they do (volume)
   - EPA/efficiency already derived from volume stats
   - Added complexity without new information
   - Result: 4.66 MAE (same as V2, 0% improvement)

3. WHY V4 WORKS EXPLAINED
   - Vegas lines predict VOLUME and OPPORTUNITY
   - High team total = more plays = more touches
   - Spread predicts game script:
     * Trailing = more passing (QB/WR/TE boost)
     * Leading = more rushing (RB boost)
   - Over/under predicts pace (fast = more plays)
   - Context (Vegas) beats analytics (EPA)
   - Result: 4.26 MAE (9% better than V2, 17% vs V1)

4. POSITION-SPECIFIC HYPERPARAMETERS JUSTIFIED
   - QB scoring is complex (4 vs 6 pt TDs, 0.04 vs 0.1 yards)
   - QB needs depth=9 to capture game script interactions
   - K scoring is simple (3 per FG, 1 per PAT)
   - K needs depth=3 to prevent overfitting
   - TE scoring same as WR but role varies (blocker vs receiver)
   - TE needs moderate depth=6 for role classification
   - Position complexity matches model complexity

5. TOUCHDOWNS ARE THE VARIANCE WILDCARD
   - 1 TD = 6 points = 60 yards of value!
   - Hardest stat to predict (goal-line randomness)
   - Why R² can't exceed ~0.50 in fantasy
   - Confidence intervals critical for TD variance
   - Wide interval = boom/bust player (DK Metcalf)
   - Narrow interval = consistent player (Travis Kelce)
